# Teruvion Strategic Positioning

## Core Position

Teruvion should not become another heavy infrastructure platform.

It should become a **Digital Earth intelligence orchestration layer** above data, model, paper, compute, storage, and communication platforms.

In short:

> Externalize heavy infrastructure. Own the semantic layer, evidence layer, orchestration layer, trust layer, and user-intelligence layer.

## What Teruvion Should Not Own

Teruvion should avoid direct competition with mature infrastructure platforms unless the capability is a direct source of intelligence.

External platforms should handle:

- large Earth data storage and computation: Google Earth Engine, Copernicus CDS, NASA Earthdata, AWS Open Data, Microsoft Planetary Computer
- model and dataset hosting: Hugging Face, GitHub, Zenodo, Figshare
- long-term research archiving and DOI records: Zenodo, Figshare, institutional repositories
- object storage: Cloudflare R2, S3-compatible storage, Supabase Storage
- authentication, email, and payment rails: Supabase, Clerk, Resend, Postmark, Stripe, Paddle
- low-level search and vector infrastructure: Postgres, pgvector, Qdrant, Typesense, Meilisearch
- map basemaps and rendering infrastructure: MapLibre, Mapbox, OSM-compatible providers

Teruvion should connect to these systems, not duplicate them.

## What Teruvion Must Own

Teruvion's durable value should come from the layers that external platforms cannot provide on its behalf:

- **Digital Earth object graph**: sources, capabilities, regions, variables, risks, resources, models, events, workflows, evidence, actions, and user interests as connected objects
- **source decomposition engine**: source admission, ontology activation, object extraction, relation extraction, provenance, and coverage audit
- **capability-to-world bridge**: mapping data, models, methods, and workflows to Earth processes, variables, regions, risks, and actions
- **evidence and provenance layer**: source traces, claim support, uncertainty, reproducibility, coverage, and audit state
- **user interest graph**: watchlists, tracked regions, tracked risks, preferred evidence, feedback, and personalization state
- **orchestration layer**: deciding when to call external data, model, compute, storage, search, and notification systems, then turning results back into trustworthy Digital Earth objects

## Product Implication

Teruvion should not lead with:

- upload your dataset
- host your model
- store your files
- summarize this paper

It should lead with:

- connect your sources
- connect your models and datasets
- decompose research into Digital Earth objects
- build an evidence-backed object graph
- watch a region, topic, resource, risk, or model
- receive personalized, traceable intelligence updates
- audit why a result or recommendation matters

## Commercial Logic

Teruvion's commercial value is not traditional hosting and not generic AI summarization.

It becomes valuable when it creates **ongoing dependency**:

- users rely on it to monitor important source changes
- teams rely on it as a shared research object workspace
- analysts rely on it to produce evidence-backed reports
- organizations rely on it to track regions, risks, resources, models, and uncertainty
- enterprises rely on it to connect internal data and models with external scientific evidence

The core paid value is:

- continuous monitoring
- personalized intelligence
- evidence-backed recommendations
- model/data/risk orchestration
- team knowledge assets
- decision support with provenance

## Likely Customer Segments

Early users and paying users should be separated.

Early users may include:

- individual researchers
- PhD students and postdocs
- research engineers
- independent Earth science practitioners

They are useful for feedback, demos, community, and product shaping, but they are unlikely to be the main revenue base.

More realistic early paying users include:

- research labs and PI-led teams
- Earth science, hydrology, climate, and environmental data groups
- environmental and climate-risk consultancies
- water, energy, agriculture, infrastructure, and insurance teams with region-based risk exposure
- government, institutional, and international organization pilots

## Payment Triggers

Users are more likely to pay when missing information has real cost.

Strong payment triggers include:

- saving expert analysis time
- reducing the risk of missing important updates
- turning scientific sources into actionable intelligence
- maintaining a trusted evidence chain
- coordinating models, datasets, regions, and risks across a team
- generating recurring reports, alerts, and audit-ready explanations

Weak payment triggers include:

- one-off paper summaries
- generic document upload
- decorative graphs without evidence
- non-personalized alerts
- recommendations that cannot explain source, uncertainty, or action relevance

## Strategic Boundary

Teruvion should sell the ability to continuously understand Earth-related change, not the ability to host files.

The strategic claim is:

> Teruvion is the Digital Earth intelligence layer above data, models, papers, and compute platforms.
